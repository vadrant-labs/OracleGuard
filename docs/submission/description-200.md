**OracleGuard — policy-governed Cardano treasury disbursements with live oracle pricing and offline verification**

Treasuries on Cardano commonly release funds via multi-sig or ad-hoc scripts — neither gives an outside party a way to check that the release actually followed the rules. OracleGuard binds disbursement to an anchored public policy plus a live Charli3 ADA/USD fact: the policy's release cap shifts by band depending on current price, and a deterministic three-gate closure (anchor → registry → grant) runs inside a 4-node BFT network, producing a canonical `AuthorizationResult` under a quorum certificate. On *Authorized*, a real Cardano Preprod transaction settles exactly the authorized amount — nothing else. On *Denied*, no transaction exists by design.

Every decision emits a canonical evidence bundle — intent, authorization, execution — that anyone can feed to the public offline verifier in this repo and reproduce the verdict without trusting the network. Because policy identity is a 32-byte sha256 of canonical JSON, any rule change is observable as a new `policy_ref`. OracleGuard's scope is narrow by design: it is not a policy engine, decision engine, or verification platform — it is the thin domain adapter connecting Cardano treasuries to a deterministic consensus layer, making governed disbursements byte-reproducible, auditable, and independently verifiable.
